Discovery of exoplanets

Astronomers continue to discover new exoplanets, some of which are potentially habitable. Recent discoveries include TOI-700 d, a potentially habitable exoplanet located about 100 light-years away from Earth, and Proxima Centauri b, an exoplanet orbiting the closest star to our solar system, Proxima Centauri

The black hole

Astronomers have identified the most massive stellar black hole yet discovered in the Milky Way galaxy. This black hole was spotted in data from the European Space Agency's Gaia mission because it imposes an odd 'wobbling' motion on the companion star orbiting it. Astronomers have verified the mass of the black hole, putting it at an impressive 33 times that of the Sun

Gravitational Waves

One notable development was the continued detection of gravitational waves from merging black holes and neutron stars by observatories like LIGO (Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory) and Virgo These detections have provided unprecedented insights into the nature of black holes, neutron stars, and the dynamics of extreme events in the universe

Interstellar Objects

The discovery of 'Oumuamua in 2017, the first confirmed interstellar object detected passing through our solar system, has sparked renewed interest in studying such objects. Astronomers are developing new techniques to detect and characterize interstellar visitors, which provide valuable insights into the conditions and dynamics of other star systems

Dark Matter and Dark Energy

Understanding the nature of dark matter and dark energy, which together make up the vast majority of the universe's mass-energy content, remains a fundamental challenge in modern cosmology. Observations from telescopes and experiments on Earth continue to provide clues about their properties and roles in shaping the cosmos

International Space Station (ISS)

Astronauts aboard the ISS continue to conduct experiments to understand the effects of long-duration spaceflight on the human body. This research is crucial for planning future missions to destinations like Mars, where astronauts will need to live for extended periods
