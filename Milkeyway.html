Telescope
A telescope is an optical instrument designed to make distant objects appear closer and more detailed. It works by collecting and magnifying light, allowing astronomers to observe celestial bodies such as stars, planets, and galaxies. Telescopes have revolutionized our understanding of the universe by providing a deeper look into space.

Uranus
Uranus is an ice giant with a tilted axis, causing it to rotate on its side. It has a pale blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere.
Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet, with a turbulent atmosphere marked by colorful bands and the famous Great Red Spot, a massive storm.
Neptune
Neptune is another ice giant, known for its deep blue color and strong winds, making it one of the windiest places in the solar system.
Venus
Venus is often called Earth's twin due to its similar size, but it has a thick, toxic atmosphere and is the hottest planet in the solar system.
Mercury
Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun, with extreme temperature variations between its scorching day and freezing night.
Sun
The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system, providing the essential light and heat that sustain life on Earth. Composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, it generates energy through nuclear fusion in its core. The Sun's influence extends far beyond its immediate surroundings, driving weather patterns, climate, and the orbits of all the planets.
