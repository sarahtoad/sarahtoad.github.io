WHAT IS ASTRONOMY ?

THE SCIENCE OF THE UNIVERSE

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects such as stars, planets, comets, and galaxies, as well as phenomena that originate outside Earth's atmosphere. It is one of the oldest natural sciences, as people have always been fascinated by the night sky.

It combines four domains...

Observational Astronomy

Using telescopes, it observes celestial objects' positions, motions, and properties.

Read more

Theoretical Astronomy

Develops mathematical models and theories, explaining phenomena with physics and mathematics.

Read more

Astrophysics

Merges astronomy and physics, studying celestial objects, physical properties and dynamics.

Read more

Cosmology

Studies the universe's origin, evolution, fate, and topics as the Big Bang, dark matter, and energy.

Read more
SINCE WHEN?

ONE OF THE OLDEST SCIENCES

Throughout history, astronomy has been a journey of discovery, from early civilizations mapping the stars to modern space exploration. Pioneering astronomers like Copernicus and Galileo revolutionized our understanding, laying the groundwork for space missions that continue to unveil the mysteries of the universe today.
